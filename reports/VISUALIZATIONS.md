# Visualization and Reporting Documentation

## Overview
This folder contains the final visualizations and reports generated from the Sales Department attrition analysis.

## Files Included

### Human Resources Data _ Module 6  revised.xlsx
This Excel workbook contains the comprehensive dashboard and visualizations for the HR attrition analysis.

## Dashboard: Salespeople with Attrition vs Non-Attrition

### Key Visualization
The primary dashboard presents a clear comparison of attrition patterns within the Sales Department.

### Main Metrics Displayed

| Category | Count | Percentage |
|----------|-------|------------|
| **Attrition** | 92 | 20.6% |
| **Non-Attrition** | 354 | 79.4% |
| **Total** | 446 | 100% |

### Visualizations Included

1. **Attrition Overview**
   - Visual comparison of employees who left vs. stayed
   - Clear segmentation showing the 92:354 split

2. **Income Analysis Charts**
   - Monthly income distributions for both groups
   - Statistical comparisons (average, min, max)
   - Income bracket analysis

3. **Job Satisfaction Correlations**
   - Relationship between job satisfaction and attrition
   - Satisfaction levels across different income ranges

## Key Insights from Visualizations

1. **Attrition Rate**: Approximately 1 in 5 salespeople (20.6%) experienced attrition

2. **Income Patterns**: Visual analysis reveals potential correlations between income levels and likelihood of attrition

3. **Retention Success**: 79.4% retention rate indicates strong overall employee retention

## How to Use These Reports

1. Open the Excel file to view interactive dashboards
2. Use filters and slicers to explore different segments
3. Review charts to identify patterns and trends
4. Compare statistics across attrition groups

## Report Purpose
These visualizations support data-driven decision-making for:
- HR retention strategies
- Compensation planning
- Employee satisfaction initiatives
- Sales Department workforce planning
